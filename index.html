How It Works

A process built to remove uncertainty.

Most people have never hired a builder before — and the industry doesn't make it easy. Here's exactly what working with us looks like, start to finish.

  1. 1Initial Consultation

    A no-pressure conversation — by phone or in person — about what you're imagining, your rough budget, and your timeline. You'll leave knowing whether the project is realistic.

  2. 2Site or Property Review

    We walk your lot or home, looking at the things that quietly drive cost: slope, drainage, access, structure, and utilities. No surprises later that were visible now.

  3. 3Scope & Preliminary Budget

    A written scope and realistic budget range — before design money is spent. We'll flag where costs are most likely to move and what decisions drive them.

  4. 4Design & Preconstruction

    Plans, selections, and engineering coordinated with decision deadlines that protect your schedule. The goal: every expensive decision made on paper, not in the field.

  5. 5Proposal & Agreement

    A clear written proposal: scope, price, allowances, schedule, and how changes are handled. You'll understand what you're signing — we'll walk every line with you.

  6. 6Construction & Progress Updates

    Regular updates on schedule and budget, quality checkpoints at every stage before work gets covered, and a clean, organized jobsite your neighbors won't resent.

  7. 7Final Walkthrough & Closeout

    A detailed walkthrough together, a punch list completed promptly, and your documentation package. Then a phone that still gets answered after we're paid.

Common Questions

Answers before you even ask.

What areas do you serve?

All of Northwest Arkansas — Fayetteville, Bentonville, Rogers, Springdale, Bella Vista, Cave Springs, Centerton, Lowell, Prairie Grove, Farmington, Siloam Springs, and nearby communities.

What types of projects do you take on?

Custom homes, renovations, remodels, additions, and preconstruction planning. If you're unsure whether your project fits, a quick phone call will sort it out.

How early should I contact a builder?

Earlier than you think — ideally before you buy a lot or finalize plans. The cheapest problems to fix are the ones caught during planning, and early feasibility review is one of the most valuable things we do.

Do you help with plans, design, engineering, and permits?

Yes — we coordinate design, engineering, and permitting as part of preconstruction, and we're glad to work alongside an architect or designer you've already chosen.

Can I use my own architect or plans?

Absolutely. We'll review your existing plans, flag anything that affects cost or constructability, and build from them.

How is the preliminary budget developed?

From a written scope, current local costs, and your site's specifics — not a generic square-foot number. We'll also tell you which parts of the budget are firm and which could move, and what decisions control them.

How are change orders handled?

In writing, before the work happens. You'll see the cost and schedule impact of any change and approve it first — no surprise invoices at the end.

How often will I get updates during construction?

On a regular schedule you can count on, plus immediately whenever something needs your decision. You'll always know where the project stands, and you talk directly with the owner.

How long does a typical project take?

It depends on scope — a renovation and a full custom home are very different animals. What we commit to: a realistic schedule up front, honest updates when anything shifts, and no schedule promises we can't keep.

What happens during the first consultation?

We listen. You tell us what you're imagining, we ask questions about how you live, your budget comfort zone, and your timeline — and we give you an honest read on feasibility. No pressure, no obligation, no fee.
